Project Guidelines


Inspiration

These project guidelines have been heavily adapted from related projects for the following courses:

  • University of Virginia (Spring 2021) CS 3501: “Ethics for Computer Science”
  • Stanford University (Spring 2020) CS 181: “Computers, Ethics, and Public Policy”

Requirements

Students will work on a project over the semester. These projects are designed to engage students with the real-world impacts of ethical work in technology. The project must produce a deliverable that engages with the outside world regarding an issue of ethics. An ideal project would concern a problem that is not specific to the UVA community, but exceptional ideas that are specifically impactful on grounds will be considered. Students have a lot of flexibility to choose their medium of delivery. Projects could take the form of a blog post, website, video, or computing artifact. However, project teams must quantify, explain, or demonstrate the impact of their project within the community.

Logistics

Projects may be completed individually or with (up to 4) additional team members. Any teams will need to submit one document per team. Please note: the amount of work done for the project is expected to scale with group size, so there is no grading “advantage” for larger groups.  

There have been three Discord channels designed for project-related conversations:

  • #idea-brainstorm will be used for initial brainstorming of ideas and project deliverables
  • #partner-search will be used for students with an idea to find additional group members
  • #proposal will be a chance for teams to share their approved project proposals

Grading Breakdown

(% of overall course grade shown below)

  • Project Proposal (5%)
  • Midpoint (5%)
  • Written Deliverable (10%)
  • Presentation (10%)

Important Deadlines

All project-related assignments are due *before class* on Wednesday of the specified week. As a reminder to students, these deadlines will also be on the Collab assignments page and the course schedule.

  • Week 4: Project proposal
  • Week 7: Midpoint
  • Week 12: Write-up
  • Week 13: Presentation

Project Proposal

The project proposal (around 500 words) should give a brief overview of the ethical issues being considered. Students should describe the type of project deliverable they will be producing and how it will impact the larger community. For larger teams, the group should describe how each team member will actively contribute to the project. It would be helpful for teams to include a brief description of published work related to the proposed project. Any projects that build upon or modify an existing artifact should give explicit credit to the original creator and justify the proposed modifications.

Midpoint

Students should report on their project progress. Specifically, what concrete steps have been taken towards completing the project? The midpoint report should demonstrate substantial progress towards the production of an impactful deliverable. If any circumstances have changed since the project proposal, students should document the changes to their plan and strategies going forward.

Please note: this is not a description of how much progress has been made on the write-up. The write-up is a reflection on the impact and success of the project deliverable itself.

Write-Up

The final write-up should include a comprehensive overview of the ethical issue being considered, why it has a significant impact, and why the team chose that topic. Any relevant works considered during the previous stages of the project should be described and appropriately cited. There should be a clear description of the intended project deliverable and the intended impact on the larger community. If applicable, groups can include screenshots, code snippets, links, or supplementary material that helps the reader understand the project deliverable. Teams should reflect on their project’s outcomes and how the intended impact compares to the actual implementation.

Presentation

Students should prepare a 5-10 minute presentation that is engaging, interactive, and clearly demonstrates their project’s value. The presentation will be given to other students in the class. Teams should strive to present their material creatively, and the best presentations will be rewarded with a bonus prize at the end of the semester.
